1 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: Stable frozen sea ice to hunt, live and raise their young.

Paragraph 1 states Arctic animals "rely entirely on stable ice to live, hunt and raise their young."

北極動物完全依賴穩定的海冰來生存、捕獵和養育後代。

[1] ...rely entirely on stable ice to live, hunt and raise their young.

2 Multiple Choice 1 mark

Answer: B — special body features for survival

"Adaptations" refers to physical features that help animals survive in extreme cold.

"Adaptations" 指幫助動物在極寒環境中生存的特殊身體特徵。

3 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: They spend almost their whole lives travelling and hunting on floating Arctic sea ice.

Paragraph 2: "because they spend most of their lives travelling across floating sea ice."

北極熊大部分時間在浮冰上生活和捕獵。

[2] Polar bears are classified as marine mammals, because they spend most of their lives travelling across floating sea ice.

4 True / False / Not Given 3 marks

(i) False   (ii) True   (iii) False

(i) Tusks are "grown by male individuals" (para 3). (ii) "change their fur colour with the seasons" (para 4). (iii) Oil spills "poison all marine life" (para 5).

(i) 假——只有雄性才有長牙 (ii) 真——毛色隨季節變化 (iii) 假——油污毒害所有海洋生物。

5 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: Burning fossil fuels for energy and transport; industrial Arctic oil/gas drilling

Paragraph 5 lists two major human activities.

第5段列出兩項人類活動。

[5] Burning fossil fuels... industrial oil and gas drilling in the Arctic damages ice surfaces and creates toxic spills.

6 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: To compete against other male narwhals and detect changes in ocean water

Paragraph 3: "to compete for female narwhals or sense changes in ocean water."

用來競爭雌性或感知海水變化。

[3] ...males use it to compete for female narwhals or sense changes in ocean water.

7 Find Word 1 mark

Answer: camouflage

Paragraph 4: "change their fur colour with the seasons for natural camouflage."

第4段提到北極狐和雪兔隨季節改變毛色以達到偽裝效果。

[4] ...change their fur colour with the seasons for natural camouflage.

8 Summary — ONE word each 4 marks

(i) camouflage   (ii) drilling   (iii) emissions   (iv) habitats

(i) para 4 (ii) para 5 (iii) para 7 (iv) para 7

各詞均須出自文中。

9 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: International treaties limit polar fossil fuel extraction; scientists run ice observation research projects.

Paragraph 6 lists: signed agreements and ice observation missions.

第6段列出：各國簽署協議和科學家進行冰層觀測。

[6] Many nations signed agreements... Scientists also carry out long-term ice observation missions.

10 Difference & Similarity 2 marks

(i) Difference: Polar bears hunt seals as main food; foxes eat leftover meat from bear kills.
(ii) Similarity: Both grow thick fur to survive extreme Arctic cold.

Difference from paras 2 & 4. Similarity: both have thick fur.

差異：北極熊主動捕獵，北極狐吃剩肉。相似：兩者都有厚毛皮。

11 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: Bears become trapped on land without access to food and slowly starve.

Paragraph 2: "bears get trapped on land with no way to find prey and slowly starve."

北極熊被困在陸地上無法捕獵，慢慢餓死。

[2] ...bears get trapped on land with no way to find prey and slowly starve.

12 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: Cut household electricity waste; walk/cycle instead of driving cars; choose wind/solar powered brands.

Paragraph 7 lists three habits.

第7段列出三項習慣。

[7] Reduce electricity waste at home, choose walking or cycling over private car rides, and support brands that use 100% renewable wind or solar power.

13 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: They eat leftover seal meat scraps left by polar bears after hunting.

Paragraph 4: "Foxes follow polar bears to feed on leftover seal meat scraps."

北極狐吃北極熊剩下的海豹肉碎。

[4] Foxes follow polar bears to feed on leftover seal meat scraps...

14 Multiple Choice 1 mark

Answer: B — Generally hopeful

The writer acknowledges threats but also presents solutions. The tone is concerned but hopeful.

作者承認威脅但也提出解決方案，語氣關切但抱有希望。

15 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: The Arctic frozen ice environment is rapidly disappearing and all polar wildlife faces survival risks.

The title uses "Save Our" which implies urgency.

標題使用「Save Our」暗示北極棲息地正面臨危險。
