🔑 Solution & Explanations — Could we ditch fast fashion?

Total marks: 20 marks

1 Phrase Meaning 1 mark

It means fashion brands constantly search for manufacturers who can produce clothes at lower costs than their competitors to keep prices low for shoppers.

"Chase the cheapest needle" → needle represents manufacturing/labor, so brands chase lowest production costs.

"Chase the cheapest needle" 意思就是品牌不斷尋找能夠以更低成本生產衣服的工廠，來保持售價低廉。

[1] Companies chase the cheapest needle, meaning they constantly hunt manufacturers who can cut costs lower than competitors, driving clothing prices down for shoppers.

2 Vocabulary 1 mark

Answer: B — abandon

The title asks "Could we ditch fast fashion?" → "ditch" means to get rid of/stop using/abandon something harmful. Context matches: it asks if we can give up fast fashion.

標題問能不能放棄快時尚，ditch 意思就是放棄/拋棄，所以選 B abandon。

Title: Could we ditch fast fashion?

3 True / False / Not Given 3 marks

i) T   ii) F   iii) NG

i) True → "This business model boomed in the 1960s" → matches statement.
ii) False → "the resale market will double the size of fast fashion by 2030... Online resale platforms... celebrities now promote thrifting" → many people do accept/buy second-hand, so statement "Most refuse" is false.
iii) Not Given → The text only says synthetic fabrics take 200 years to decompose, never mentions how long natural cotton takes → NG.

i) 對——文中說快時尚模式在1960年代蓬勃發展，符合聲明。
ii) 錯——文中說二手市場會在2030翻倍，名人也推廣二手衣，所以不是大部分人拒絕穿，錯。
iii) 未提及——文中只說合成纖維要200年分解，從未說天然棉布多久分解，所以NG。

[1] This business model boomed in the 1960s... [3] ...Synthetic fabrics do not break down easily, so millions of unwanted clothes end up in landfills where they take up to 200 years to decompose. [5] Reports predict the resale market will double the size of fast fashion by 2030.

4 Short Answer 2 marks

Invisible social pressure from social media: people feel they need new outfits for online photos and avoid repeating the same clothes publicly.

Direct answer from paragraph 2: social media creates pressure to always wear new clothes for photos.

第二段直接回答：社交媒體帶來看不見的社會壓力，人們覺得需要新衣服拍照，避免在網上重複穿同一件衣服。

[2] Many people feel invisible social pressure from social media to buy new outfits for photos; they avoid repeating the same clothes online...

5 Short Answer 2 marks

(Any two valid answers below):
1. It wastes huge amounts of water, and toxic factory dyes poison rivers/water supplies.
2. Synthetic fabrics create massive landfill waste that takes centuries to decompose.
3. The fashion industry produces more carbon emissions than international flights and sea shipping combined.

Any two damages from paragraph 3 will get full marks.

第三段提到多種環境傷害，任舉兩種即可得分：水資源浪費、有毒染料污染河流、合成纖維在堆填區幾百年不分解、時裝業碳排放比航運和飛機更多。

[3] ...A single cotton T-shirt requires 2,700 litres of water to produce. Factories dump toxic dye chemicals into local rivers, poisoning wildlife and drinking water supplies. Synthetic fabrics do not break down easily... The fashion industry's total carbon output exceeds international flights and sea shipping combined.

6 Short Answer 1 mark

The 2013 Rana Plaza factory collapse in Bangladesh.

Directly stated in the text: this disaster killed over 1,100 workers and exposed unsafe working conditions.

文中直接說：2013年孟加拉國 Rana Plaza 廠房倒塌，超過1100名工人死亡，暴露了惡劣工作環境。

[4] The 2013 Rana Plaza factory collapse in Bangladesh killed over 1,100 garment workers, exposing unsafe buildings, extremely long shifts and tiny wages for staff.

7 Reference 1 mark

It refers to (the big) fast fashion clothing brands.

Previous sentence: "Many brands ignore worker rights to keep their production expenses minimal..." → "their" refers back to "many brands".

前文：「Many brands ignore worker rights to keep their production expenses minimal」，所以 their 指代這些大快時尚服裝品牌。

[4] Many brands ignore worker rights to keep their production expenses minimal, despite public campaigns demanding fair pay and safe workplaces.

8 One-word Summary 4 marks

(i) market   (ii) repair   (iii) circular   (iv) greenwashing

All answers directly taken from the passage:

所有答案直接從文中提取：
(i) 「Reports predict the resale market will double the size of fast fashion by 2030」 → (i)
(ii) 「Experts... argue people should repair and revamp their existing wardrobes」 → (ii)
(iii) 「Some brands also launch circular fashion lines: these items are built from biodegradable materials」 → (iii)
(iv) 「However, shoppers must watch out for "greenwashing"」 → (iv)

All words found directly in paragraphs 5-7 matching the summary.

9 Short Answer 2 marks

Because celebrities now promote buying second-hand clothes (thrifting) on mainstream TV shows, which changes the old view that second-hand clothes are only for people with little money.

Direct answer: celebrities promoting thrifting on TV made second-hand clothing more socially acceptable.

名人現在會在主流電視節目推廣買二手衣（撿便宜），改變了二手衣只有窮人才穿的舊觀念，所以大眾看法改變。

[5] ...celebrities now promote thrifting on mainstream TV shows, changing the old idea that second-hand clothes are only for people with little money.

10 Short Answer 2 marks

(Any two of these):
1. Repairing and revamping your existing old clothes before buying new ones.
2. Borrowing clothes from clothing libraries instead of buying.
3. Buying circular fashion lines made from biodegradable recycled materials.

Paragraph 6 introduces these three sustainable solutions, any two get full marks.

第六段中介紹這三個可持續方案，任舉兩個即可得分：修理翻新舊衣、從衣物圖書館借衣服不要買、買可生物降解循環時裝。

[6] Experts... argue people should repair and revamp their existing wardrobes before purchasing anything new. Clothing libraries let customers borrow premium outfits instead of buying them. Some brands also launch circular fashion lines: these items are built from biodegradable materials and designed to be recycled once they wear out.

11 Difference & Similarity 2 marks

i) Difference: Circular fashion items are made from biodegradable materials and designed to be recycled after use, while ordinary fast fashion clothes use synthetic materials that do not decompose easily.
ii) Similarity: Both are produced and sold by fashion brands to consumers who want clothing.

Difference: materials and design for recycling → circular is sustainable, fast fashion is not.
Similarity: both are business models to sell clothing to shoppers.

差異：循環時裝用可生物降解材料，設計成用完可回收，而普通快時尚用不降解合成材料。
相似：二者都是服裝品牌生產賣給消費者。

[6] Some brands also launch circular fashion lines: these items are built from biodegradable materials and designed to be recycled once they wear out.

12 Short Answer 2 marks

Because big brands only release one small eco collection each year while still producing thousands of new fast fashion items monthly. They use vague words like "eco" without any clear proof of real environmental change, so they trick buyers into thinking they are ethical.

Greenwashing means pretending to be eco-friendly without actually making meaningful changes to deceive consumers.

大廠商每年只出一個小型環保系列，但每月仍然生產成千上萬件新快時尚衣服。他們只用 "eco" 這種模糊字眼，沒有真正改變的明確證據，誤導消費者以為他們支持道德生產。

[7] Many big fashion labels release one small eco-friendly capsule collection each year while producing thousands of new fast fashion pieces monthly. They use vague words like "eco" or "sustainable" without clear proof of real change, tricking buyers into believing they support ethical production.

13 Inference 2 marks

The writer implies that these small eco collections are just for marketing (greenwashing) and do not fix the core environmental and ethical harm caused by their large-scale fast fashion production.

The writer puts "greenwashing" in quotes and explains that brands trick buyers, so he implies the small collections don't solve the real problem.

作者指出這就是「漂綠」，品牌只是欺騙消費者，小環保系列只是營銷手法，並未解決大規模快時尚生產帶來的核心環境和道德傷害。

[7] However, shoppers must watch out for "greenwashing". Many big fashion labels release one small eco-friendly capsule collection each year while producing thousands of new fast fashion pieces monthly. They use vague words... tricking buyers into believing they support ethical production.

14 Attitude 1 mark

Answer: C — Critical and concerned

The writer spends most of the text explaining the severe environmental and human harms of fast fashion, and talks about solutions to replace it. This shows he is critical and concerned about the problem.

作者大部分篇幅解釋快時尚帶來嚴重環境和人力傷害，並談論替代方案，顯示他對快時尚批評且關心問題，所以選C。

Whole text: highlights severe environmental and human costs of fast fashion, promotes sustainable alternatives.

15 Inference 2 marks

It suggests that most people currently still rely heavily on cheap fast fashion for their clothing, but it is harmful and we have the possibility to change our habits and stop buying it in the future.

The phrasing "Could we..." implies that it is a question asking if it is possible for us to give up fast fashion, meaning we currently still use it a lot.

標題用 "Could we..." 疑問句，表示現在大多數人仍然嚴重依賴廉價快時尚，但快時尚有害，我們有可能改變習慣放棄它。

Title: Could we ditch fast fashion?
