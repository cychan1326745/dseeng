1 Three-part Multiple Choice 3 marks

(i) B   (ii) B   (iii) C

Line 1: "People have been enjoying stories for as long as humanity has been around" → (i) matches B "as old as". "while others struggle to read the first four paragraphs" → struggle means find it difficult → (ii) B. "Flash fiction is a medium that works perfectly..." → it is the ideal solution for readers with short attention spans → (iii) C.

第一句說故事和人類歷史一樣久，"as long as humanity has been around" → "as old as" 同義，所以選B。"others struggle to read..." → struggle 即感到困難，所以選B difficult。"works perfectly in a low-attention-span world" → 對現在讀者來說是理想方案，所以選C。

[1] People have been enjoying stories for as long as humanity has been around... while others struggle to read the first four paragraphs... Flash fiction is a medium that works perfectly in a low-attention-span world.

2 Vocabulary 1 mark

unfortunately

Paragraph 1: "More and more, technology has unfortunately resulted in many people having shorter attention spans." The word "unfortunately" shows the writer feels disappointed/unhappy about this change.

第一段說 "technology has unfortunately resulted..."，副詞 "unfortunately" 顯示作者對人們注意力變短這個改變感到失望和不開心，所以答案是 unfortunately。

[1] More and more, technology has unfortunately resulted in many people having shorter attention spans.

3 Short Answer 2 marks

Suggested answer: Technology has resulted in many people having shorter attention spans, so writers need new ways to appeal to these readers who are used to reading short snippets on smartphones.

The text says technology caused people to have shorter attention spans, and they are used to reading short snippets on smartphones, so writers must find new methods like flash fiction to appeal to them.

科技改變了人們的閱讀習慣，令人們注意力變短，習慣在手機閱讀短片段，所以作家需要找新方法來吸引這些讀者。

[1] More and more, technology has unfortunately resulted in many people having shorter attention spans. Therefore, writers have to find new ways to tell stories...

4 Vocabulary 1 mark

snippets

"reading snippets of information" → snippets means small/brief/concise pieces of information, which matches "things that are brief and concise".

"reading snippets of information"，snippet 意思就是短小簡潔的信息片段，符合題目要求。

[1] ...appeals to people who are used to reading snippets of information on the small screen of their smart phones.

5 Vocabulary 3 marks

(i) limit (or limits)   (ii) solid   (iii) overkill

(i) Para 2: "Flash fiction is a category of short story that limits the author to a word count of 1,000 words or less." → "limits" = restrict → (i).
(ii) "Following the tips below will guide you in writing a solid flash fiction story" → solid means strong/substantial → matches "strong".
(iii) "any more than that will probably be overkill" → overkill means more than is needed/too much of something → matches (iii).

(i) 第二段 "limits the author to 1000 words or less"，limit = restrict 限制 → 答案。
(ii) "Following the tips below will guide you in writing a solid flash fiction story"，solid 即紮實/強烈 → 同義於 strong。
(iii) "any more than three characters will probably be overkill"，overkill 即做過頭/太多 → 同義於 too much of something。

[2] Flash fiction is a category of short story that limits the author to a word count of 1,000 words or less. [3] Following the tips below will guide you in writing a solid flash fiction story. [4] ...any more than that will probably be overkill in the realm of flash fiction.

6 Error Correction 5 marks

(i) opposes / opposes the main character / antagonist
(ii) ✓
(iii) (no chance) → possibility / chance that you may
(iv) (unless → if)

(i) Original: "you’ll often use a human antagonist as opposition to your main character." Antagonist opposes the main character, so the summary says "supports" which is wrong, correct to "opposes".
(ii) "So the story will probably have two characters." → matches original statement, so correct (✓).
(iii) Original: "You might be able to use three characters." → "might" = possibility, summary says "no chance" which is wrong. Correct to "possibility".
(iv) Original: "...any more than three characters will probably be overkill..." → meaning if you have more than three, word count exceeds 1000. Summary uses "unless" which is opposite meaning, correct to "if".

(i) 原文是 antagonist 對抗主角，摘要錯寫成 supports 支持，更正為 opposes / antagonist。
(ii) 摘要說可能有兩個角色，符合原文，所以正確打勾。
(iii) 原文說你「可能」用到三個角色，摘要說「不可能」，錯誤，更正為 possibility / might。
(iv) 原文意思是「如果」你超過三個就會太長，摘要錯用 unless，更正為 if。

[4] With a limit of 1,000 words... There will likely be two characters in the story because you'll often use a human antagonist as opposition to your main character. You might be able to use three characters, but any more than that will probably be overkill...

7 One-word Blank 4 marks

(i) limited   (ii) complex   (iii) final / end   (iv) scene

(i) "your limited word count focuses on the essentials" → first blank matches "The (i) ______ length..."
(ii) "you won’t be able to flesh out a complex story world" → longer stories can develop a more complex environment.
(iii) "with flash fiction, you’re really telling only the end of a story" → the final/end part is the focus.
(iv) "your flash fiction story should focus on one scene" → exactly matches "single (iv) ______".

(i) 文中說 "your limited word count focuses on the essentials" → 有限長度，所以 (i) limited。
(ii) "you won't be able to flesh out a complex story world" → 長篇小說可以發展更複雜環境 → (ii) complex。
(iii) "you're really telling only the end of a story" → 典型故事結構的最後部分就是焦點 → (iii) end / final。
(iv) "focus on one scene" → exactly matches，所以 (iv) scene。

[5] Longer story forms have a beginning, middle, and end... Of course, you won't be able to flesh out a complex story world. Instead, your flash fiction story should focus on one scene, one moment in the life of your character. ... So for flash fiction your limited word count focuses on the essentials to the story.

8 Reference 1 mark

(Your main character's / the writer's) theme / the moral argument of your story.

Line 27 (paragraph 7): "Your theme is the moral argument of your story. It’s about how you want your readers to be impacted by the story." So "it" refers back to "your theme" / the theme of the story.

第7段第一句：「Your theme is the moral argument of your story. It's about how you want your readers to be impacted...」，所以 it 指代前一句的 theme (主題 / 道德論點)。

[7] Your theme is the moral argument of your story. It's about how you want your readers to be impacted by the story.

9 MC 1 mark

Answer: D

"It’s about how you want your readers to be impacted by the story. What do you want them to be encouraged to do, be, or believe as a result of reading your story?" → This means the moral argument influences how readers think/respond to the story → matches D.

文中說主題（道德論點）是關於你想讀者怎樣被故事影響，你想鼓勵他們做什麼、相信什麼 → 即是影響讀者對故事的反應，所以選D。

[7] It's about how you want your readers to be impacted by the story. What do you want them to be encouraged to do, be, or believe as a result of reading your story?

10 Vocabulary 1 mark

It means you review / edit / revise your writing after you finish the first draft / you go back over your work to cut unnecessary words and rework what needs changing.

The context: "Often, when you’re writing flash fiction, you’ll write more than you need. When you go back through, you’ll see what needs to be there, what can be taken out, and what needs to be reworked..." → after writing the first draft, you go back and read/edit/revise your work to remove what isn't needed.

上下文：寫完初稿後你通常會寫多了，然後 "go back through" 看看哪些要保留、哪些要刪掉、哪些要改寫 → 意思就是回頭檢查、編輯、修訂你的作品，刪掉多餘文字。

[9] Often, when you're writing flash fiction, you'll write more than you need. When you go back through, you'll see what needs to be there, what can be taken out, and what needs to be reworked, which helps you to develop your editing and revising skills.

11 NOT mentioned 1 mark

Answer: A

Paragraph 9 says:

- "It helps you to focus your ideas" → matches C (clearer focus).
- "strip away anything that isn’t essential to your story" → matches D (better ability to remove unimportant material).
A "ability to write more words" is NOT mentioned — flash fiction actually requires you to write fewer words, so A is the correct answer.

[9] Flash fiction forces you to be economical with your words. It helps you to focus your ideas and strip away anything that isn't essential to your story. Often, when you're writing flash fiction, you'll write more than you need. When you go back through... which helps you to develop your editing and revising skills.

16 True / False / Not Given 4 marks

(i) F   (ii) NG   (iii) T   (iv) T

(i) False — "Four weeks and 25 excruciating extra Chemistry lessons later..." "excruciating" means extremely painful/unpleasant → Timothy did NOT enjoy it → statement is false.
(ii) Not Given — The text says "Professor Snookhorn was a superlative scientist but little did he know that Timothy was a bigger hazard than sulphuric acid..." It never says whether Snookhorn informed Timothy about hazards → so NG.
(iii) True — "...a gaggle of eager science teachers and pushy parents, crammed into the National School of Science Laboratory..." "crammed" means packed tightly/hardly any space left → matches "hardly any space left" → statement true.
(iv) True — "...Timothy looked down at the glistening bench... without a clue of what they were or how to use them." "without a clue" means he didn't know what to do → matches statement → true.

(i) 錯——文中說 "25 excruciating extra Chemistry lessons"，excruciating 表示極度痛苦，所以 Timothy 不喜歡準備過程，聲明錯。

(iii) 對——文中說老師和家長 "crammed into" 實驗室，crammed 表示擠滿了，所以空間幾乎不夠，聲明對。

21 Open-ended Evaluation 2 marks

✓ Box 1: It IS a good example of flash fiction
(Any two pieces of evidence):
1. It focuses on one main character (Timothy) → matches the "Focus on One Character" rule.
2. It focuses on one scene (the final competition explosion) → matches the "Focus on One Scene" rule.
3. It focuses on one main conflict (will Timothy's explosion win the competition) → matches "Focus on One Conflict" rule.
4. It focuses on one clear theme (even an accidental experiment can win if you're lucky) → matches "Focus on One Theme" rule.
5. The whole story is well under 1,000 words → matches the word limit for flash fiction.
Any two of the above points are acceptable.

According to all the rules of how to write flash fiction given in the first part of the text, The Big Bang matches every key principle: it has one main character, one scene, one conflict, one theme, and is very short (well under 1000 words). Therefore it is a good example.

根據文中列出寫flash fiction的規則，The Big Bang完全符合：它只有一個主角，只聚焦一個場景（比賽爆炸那一刻），只有一個衝突，只有一個主題，而且整個故事遠少於1000字。因此它是一個好例子。任舉兩點即可。

Rules: Focus on One Character, Focus on One Scene, Focus on One Conflict, Focus on One Theme, 1000 words or less. The Big Bang matches all of these.
