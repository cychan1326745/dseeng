HKDSE English Language Paper 1 (Reading) — Part A 2024

以下為全部 23 題參考答案及詳細解釋。開放式題目可接受意思相近的答案。

Total marks: About 43 marks

1 Two-part Multiple Choice 2 marks

(i) D   (ii) B

The first paragraph says "the government’s controversial decision to cut down four century-old Chinese banyans after several similar trees fell from stone walls". Century-old means 100 years, which matches "four hundred-year-old" (D). Several similar trees already fell (collapsed) after the storm, so "fell down" (B) matches.

第一段說政府砍掉「四棵百年老榕」，百年即一百多年，與 "four hundred-year-old" (D) 相符。已經有幾棵樹在風暴後「從石牆倒塌」，所以(ii) 是 fell down (B)。

[1] ...the government’s controversial decision to cut down four century-old Chinese banyans after several similar trees fell from stone walls during recent storms.

2 Short Answer 2 marks

Suggested answer: Because the government cut down four century-old banyan trees without consulting the expert panel that advises it on tree management, and many experts and local people were angry/opposed to the decision.

Paragraph 2 says: "experts expressed anger over the Highways Department’s controversial felling... without consulting a panel advising the government on tree management." The lack of consultation and expert opposition made it controversial.

因為政府砍掉四棵百年榕樹，但沒有諮詢負責樹木管理的專家小組，引來專家不滿和憤怒，因此決定具爭議性。

[2] ...experts expressed anger over the Highways Department’s controversial felling of four century-old banyan trees in Sai Ying Pun, without consulting a panel advising the government on tree management.

3 Short Answer 2 marks

Suggested answer: After the collapse on 22 July, inspections found cracks in the wall behind the remaining trees, and these cracks were seen to have worsened over time. This made the wall and trees dangerous.

Paragraph 5 says after the 22 July collapse, cracks were found in the wall behind the remaining trees, and the cracks got worse. Since the trees are rooted in the wall, worsening cracks made the wall unstable and dangerous, so it became a problem for public safety.

第一段說在七月二十二日倒塌後，檢查發現剩餘樹木後方的石牆出現裂痕，而且裂痕不斷惡化。牆壁不穩定會連帶樹木倒塌，因此對公眾安全構成問題。

[5] But by midweek last week, cracks were found in the wall behind the trees and were seen to have worsened.

4 Reference 1 mark

Suggested answer: (The four recently cut down) banyan trees / (the severed) banyan roots (on Bonham Road)

Paragraph 7 says: "local residents, who tied balloons and messages of support to the trees’ roots, which still clung to the wall that had supported them for 100 years." "Them" refers to the (four chopped down) banyan trees / their roots that were still attached to the wall.

此句是：當地居民把氣球和支持信息綁在樹根上，這些樹根仍然依附在支撐了它們一百年的牆壁上。"them" 指被砍的榕樹（或是它們的樹根）。

Text 2 paragraph 7: ...local residents, who tied balloons and messages of support to the trees’ roots, which still clung to the wall that had supported them for 100 years.

5 True / False / Not Given 3 marks

(i) T   (ii) F   (iii) NG

(i) True — Paragraph 6 says: "We agree with the department that the trees had to be removed immediately." Tree Management Office (Development Bureau) agreed with Highways Department, so they had the same view.
(ii) False — The experts were upset after the trees were cut down, not that the trees were cut down after experts became upset. The order of events is reversed.
(iii) Not Given — Text 1 never mentions whether anyone was injured or killed by the fallen trees. We can't confirm this from the text.

(i) 對——發展局樹木管理處同意路政署說必須立即移除樹木，所以兩個部門意見一致。
(ii) 錯——專家是在砍樹之後才表達憤怒，並不是砍樹發生在專家不滿之後，次序相反。
(iii) 未提及——文中從來沒有說倒塌的樹木是否有人受傷，所以是NG。

[6] "We agree with the department that the trees had to be removed immediately."

6 Text Type 1 mark

Answer: A — A news report

Text 1 reports on a recent event (the felling of old banyan trees by the government after storm damage) and quotes the government's explanation. This matches the format of a news report reporting a current controversial event.

Text 1 報道最近發生的事件（政府風災後砍掉老榕樹），並引用政府解釋，完全符合新聞報道的體裁。

Text 1 opening: "Four trees collapsed in Hong Kong on Monday morning following a night of heavy rain. This comes after the government’s controversial decision..."

7 Sentence Completion 1 mark

transformed (by permission from the government) into a temple (after World War II).

Paragraph 1 says: "After World War II, he received permission from the government to transform the shrine into a temple." So the shrine was transformed into a temple.

根據第一段：二戰後，他得到政府允許把神龕改建為廟宇。

[1] After World War II, he received permission from the government to transform the shrine into a temple.

8 Reference 1 mark

(Banyan) trees in Hong Kong / The banyan trees (that grow everywhere in Hong Kong).

The previous sentence says: "It is no coincidence that Lam’s temple started with a banyan tree. These behemoths..." So "these" refers back to the banyan trees mentioned in the previous sentence. "Behemoth" means a large/huge thing, which matches the size of mature banyans.

前一句說林的廟宇從榕樹開始，所以 "these behemoths" 指香港的榕樹。behemoth 意思是龐然大物，符合榕樹體型。

[2] It is no coincidence that Lam’s temple started with a banyan tree. These behemoths are ubiquitous in Hong Kong...

9 Inference 2 marks

Suggested answer: Jim implies that banyan trees are extremely adaptable and good at surviving and spreading in Hong Kong's environment/climate, even without human help. They will naturally colonize the whole area if people leave.

Jim is highlighting how well banyans adapt to Hong Kong conditions. They are so good at growing and spreading here that if humans left, banyans would quickly take over the entire city. This shows their strong survival ability and ubiquity.

Jim暗示榕樹在香港環境適應力極強，即使沒有人類干預，也能很好地生存和擴散，如果人類放棄香港，幾百年內榕樹就會完全佔領整個地方，說明榕樹生命力頑強，適應力強。

[2] "If Hong Kong was abandoned it would be taken over by banyans in a few hundred years," says Jim Chi-yung...

10 Vocabulary 2 marks

(i) ubiquitous   (ii) imposing / prominent

(i) "These behemoths are ubiquitous in Hong Kong" — ubiquitous means found everywhere / seen everywhere, exactly matching the definition.
(ii) "Their imposing presence defines the cityscape" — imposing means impressive/significant because of large size, so it can be replaced by significant.

(i) 文中說榕樹在香港 "ubiquitous"，這個詞意思就是到處可見，符合 "seen everywhere"。
(ii) "Their imposing presence defines the cityscape"，imposing 表示因體型巨大而給人深刻印象，可以被 significant 替換。

[2] These behemoths are ubiquitous in Hong Kong... Their imposing presence defines the cityscape.

11 Error Correction 5 marks

(i) aerial roots
(ii) ✓
(iii) thick (woody) trunk
(iv) varied / different

(i) Original text says: "Banyan trees are immediately recognisable for their spindly aerial roots, which cast outwards in search of water and nutrition." The summary incorrectly wrote "Thin airborne branches" — correct mistake is "aerial roots".
(ii) The statement matches Jim actually counted over 1,100 banyans on Hong Kong Island stone walls — this is correct, so tick.
(iii) Original: "When a root finds a suitable source... it becomes a thick, woody trunk." Summary says "produce a thin trunk" — mistake is "thin", correct is "thick".
(iv) Original: "This is what allows the banyan to grow in varied conditions." Summary says "grow in few situations" — "few" should be replaced by "varied".

(i) 原文是 "spindly aerial roots" (細氣生根)，摘要錯寫成 "Thin airborne branches"，糾正為 aerial roots。
(ii) 原文說Jim數過島上石牆有超過1100棵榕樹，這句正確，所以打勾。
(iii) 原文說根找到水源會變成 "thick, woody trunk"，摘要說 "thin trunk"，糾正為 thick。
(iv) 原文說榕樹可以在 "varied conditions" 生長，摘要說 "few situations"，糾正為 varied / different。

[3] Banyan trees are immediately recognisable for their spindly aerial roots, which cast outwards in search of water and nutrition... When a root finds a suitable source... it becomes a thick, woody trunk. This is what allows the banyan to grow in varied conditions...

12 One-word Blank 4 marks

(i) traditional Hakka   (ii) mortar   (iii) seeds   (iv) plunge

All answers are taken directly from paragraph 4:
(i) "...built using a traditional Hakka technique..."
(ii) "...does not require mortar between stone joints."
(iii) "Seeds carried by the wind or dropped by birds find their way into the cracks." → birds drop the banyan seeds.
(iv) "As the tree grows, its roots plunge dozens of metres into the soil behind the wall."

所有答案直接從第四段提取，每格一個詞（原文單詞）。

[4] ...which was built using a traditional Hakka technique that does not require mortar between stone joints. Seeds carried by the wind or dropped by birds find their way into the cracks. As the tree grows, its roots plunge dozens of metres into the soil behind the wall.

13 Three-part MC 3 marks

(i) C   (ii) A   (iii) B

(i) "Jim loves the way the banyans form a lush green canopy across Forbes Street." Canopy means a covering/backdrop, so C "backdrop" matches best.
(ii) "...he once proposed pedestrianising the street..." Pedestrianising means making it traffic-free (no cars) so people can walk and dine outside → matches A "traffic-free".
(iii) "The government’s reception was frosty." Frosty means cold/unfriendly/negative → matches B "a negative".

(i) 原文說榕樹形成綠色 canopy (樹冠) 覆蓋街道，canopy 即背景/遮蓋，所以 backdrop (C) 最接近。
(ii) Jim 提議 pedestrianising the street，即禁止車輛，所以 traffic-free (A)。
(iii) 政府反應是 frosty，即是冷淡負面，所以 negative (B)。

[5] Jim loves the way the banyans form a lush green canopy across Forbes Street. He ... proposed pedestrianising the street ... The government’s reception was frosty.

14 Reference 1 mark

Concretng over / covering the root (to stop it from growing).

Paragraph 6: "...touching a root that had been concreted over to stop it from growing. 'See what’s happening here?' he asks... 'The people who do this, they don’t understand trees. They’re defacing heritage.' " "it" refers to the action of concreting over the root to stop its growth.

第六段：Jim摸著被水泥覆蓋阻止生長的樹根說「看看這裡發生什麼事」，所以 it 指用水泥覆蓋樹根阻止生長這件事。

[6] "See what's happening here?" he asks, touching a root that had been concreted over to stop it from growing.

15 Attitude + Reason 2 marks

Attitude: Negative
Reason: He says "They're defacing heritage" / He criticises people who do this because they don't understand trees and they are ruining a natural/historic feature.

Jim clearly has a negative attitude: he says "The people who do this, they don't understand trees. They're defacing heritage. Imagine doing this to an ancient monument." The word "defacing" means ruining the appearance of something valuable/historic, so his attitude is negative.

態度：負面 (Negative)。原因：Jim批評這樣做的人說「他們在破壞古蹟（defacing heritage）」，他認為這些人不懂樹木，破壞了自然歷史景觀。

[6] "The people who do this, they don't understand trees. They're defacing heritage. Imagine doing this to an ancient monument."

16 Reference 1 mark

It refers to the Highways Department secretly chopping down four healthy banyan trees on Bonham Road after a wall tree collapsed (as a precautionary measure).

Paragraph 7: "...a wall tree collapsed onto Bonham Road, which led the Highways Department to surreptitiously chop down four healthy banyans nearby. Their sudden removal was defended by the government as a precautionary measure, but experts like Jim said it was an unnecessary overreaction." So the phrase refers to the felling of four healthy trees.

這句指在Bonham Road一棵牆樹倒塌後，路政署秘密砍掉四棵健康的榕樹，專家認為這是不必要的過度反應。

[7] ...after days of heavy rain, a wall tree collapsed onto Bonham Road, which led the Highways Department to surreptitiously chop down four healthy banyans nearby... experts like Jim said it was an unnecessary overreaction.

17 Short Answer 2 marks

Suggested answer: Because they opposed the felling of the trees / They showed support for the trees and protested against the unnecessary cutting down of healthy old banyans by the government.

The text says the felling sparked outrage from local residents, so they tied balloons and messages of support to the roots to show they opposed the decision and supported saving the trees.

因為當地居民對砍掉健康老榕樹感到憤怒，他們綁氣球和支持信息在樹根上，表示他們反對政府這個決定，支持保護樹木。

[7] It sparked outrage from local residents, who tied balloons and messages of support to the trees' roots...

18 True / False / Not Given 3 marks

(i) F   (ii) NG   (iii) F

(i) False — Visitors write their own wish on yellow paper, they don't buy prepared wishes. "Visitors write their wish on a piece of yellow paper..."
(ii) Not Given — The text only says after a branch collapsed in 2005, worshippers were told to tie wishes to a plastic tree instead. It never mentions whether the number of worshippers has declined.
(iii) False — "shrines are often built at their base, not in their branches." So the statement is wrong.

(i) 錯——遊客自己在黃紙寫願望，不是買預製的願望。
(ii) 未提及——文中只說2005年樹枝斷後改為綁到塑料樹，從來沒說參拜人數減少。

After the weight of all the wishes caused a branch to collapse in 2005, worshippers were instructed to tie their wishes to a plastic tree instead. ...shrines are often built at their base.

19 Short Answer 2 marks

Suggested answer: Because their wood cannot be used for timber (to make furniture/building) and cannot be used for firewood, so people have no economic reason to cut them down, which allows them to survive undisturbed for hundreds of years.

Anthropologist Ng notes: "Its wood is gnarled and so cannot be used as timber. It will not flame and so cannot be used for firewood. Its very lack of useful properties ensures its invincibility and survival." Since there's no economic incentive to cut them, they are not logged and survive.

因為榕樹木材彎曲不能做木材（做家具/建築），也不能燒柴，所以人們沒有經濟動機砍伐它們，這就保證了它們可以不受干擾存活數百年。

[9] "Its wood is gnarled and so cannot be used as timber. It will not flame and so cannot be used for firewood. Its very lack of useful properties ensures its invincibility and survival,"

20 Example 2 marks

Suggested answer: When a smaller tree grows near a banyan, the banyan gradually leans towards it, wraps its branches around the smaller tree, and they eventually grow together as one tree, so even an axe cannot separate them. This embracing behaviour symbolizes love.

Li Yu the 17th century playwright observed this and described it as a symbol of love because the banyan naturally wraps around and merges with another tree, becoming inseparable, which is like the closeness of love.

根據劇作家李漁觀察：當小樹長在榕樹旁邊，榕樹會漸漸靠向小樹，用枝葉環抱小樹，最後兩樹長在一起變成一體，就算斧頭也無法分開。這種互相緊抱不可分離的行為就像愛情，所以象徵愛。

[10] "Anytime a smaller tree grows before a banyan tree, this banyan will sooner or later lean its body towards the smaller tree. After a time, it wraps its branches around the smaller tree's body, and the smaller tree gradually falls into the banyan's embrace. The two trees become one, so that even if you cut them with an axe you would not be able to separate them."

22 Inference 2 marks

Suggested answer: The writer is referring to banyan trees' extraordinary ability to survive and grow even in very difficult conditions / They can survive even after being cut down (new sprouts grow from the stumps).

The last sentence says: "And while the future of many old and prominent banyans is contentious you can count on banyans to find a way to survive even in the most difficult conditions. New sprouts are already growing from the stumps of the severed banyan trees... There is a reason they are worshipped, after all." So the "reason" is their strong perseverance/survival ability even against human cutting.

最後一段說：縱使很多老榕樹未來充滿爭議，但榕樹就算在最惡劣條件下也能找到生存方法，就算被砍斷也能從樹樁長出新芽。因此作者說「它們被崇拜是有原因的」，指的就是榕樹頑強的生存能力。

[12] And while the future of many old and prominent banyans is contentious you can count on banyans to find a way to survive even in the most difficult conditions. New sprouts are already growing from the stumps of the severed banyan trees on Bonham Road. There is a reason they are worshipped, after all.
