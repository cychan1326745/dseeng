HKDSE English Language Paper 1 (Reading) — Part A

以下為全部 21 題參考答案及詳細解釋。開放式題目可接受意思相近的答案。

Total marks: 42 marks

1 Multiple Choice 1 mark

Answer: A — intends to divorce his wife.

Paragraph 1 states that "Joe Jonas has filed for divorce from Sophie Turner." Filing for divorce means he intends to end the marriage legally. The other options are not supported by paragraph 1.

第一段明確指出 Joe Jonas「已申請離婚」(filed for divorce)，即有意與妻子離婚。B、C、D 在文中均無根據。

[1] It's 2023 and Joe Jonas has filed for divorce from Sophie Turner after four years of marriage.

2 Short Answer 2 marks

Suggested answer: Because so many high-profile celebrity couples split up / called it quits in 2023.

The phrase "The Year of Celebrity Breakups" is used because "so many high-profile pairs have called it quits." The writer lists multiple celebrity breakups (Joe Jonas, Taylor Swift, Ariana Grande, Hugh Jackman, etc.) to show this was a notable trend in 2023.

2023 被稱為「名人分手年」，是因為「太多高調情侶/夫妻選擇分手」(called it quits)。文中列舉多對名人離婚或分手作為例證。

[1] …so many high-profile pairs have called it quits that some people have deemed 2023 'The Year of Celebrity Breakups.'

3 Find TWO phrases 2 marks

(i) split up   (ii) called it quits

Both phrases in paragraph 1 refer to ending a relationship. "Split up" describes Taylor Swift, Ariana Grande and others; "called it quits" is used for high-profile pairs in general.

兩個詞組均指「分手/結束關係」：split up（分手）及 called it quits（宣告結束）。

[1] …all split up. In fact, so many high-profile pairs have called it quits…

4 Reference 2 marks

Suggested answer: Ordinary people / the general public / us (non-celebrities)

"Down here in the 'real' world" contrasts celebrities' lives with those of ordinary readers. The writer means people like us who live everyday lives, as opposed to the glamorous celebrity world.

「在這個『真實』世界裡」與名人的光鮮生活形成對比，指的是普通大眾/讀者/我們這些非名人。

[2] …their romantic relationships have no bearing on how our own lives unravel, down here in the 'real' world.

5 Pronoun Reference 2 marks

(i) being interested in celebrity gossip / our interest in gossip
(ii) finding out that celebrities are getting cheated on, divorcing or breaking up

(i) Line 8: "And yet we are interested, even if we're reluctant to admit it" — "it" refers to being interested in celebrity gossip.
(ii) Line 14 in paragraph 3: "when we find out that… celebrities are… breaking up, it kind of humanizes them" — "it" refers to discovering celebrities' relationship problems.

(i) 第8行：「it」= 對名人八卦感興趣（即使不願承認）。
(ii) 第3段：「it」= 發現名人被出軌、離婚或分手這件事。

6 Short Answer 2 marks

Suggested answer: Because celebrities often perform a happy image online that does not match their real lives / the public only saw a happy family.

Dr. Liner explains that celebrities may perform an image that doesn't align with their authentic selves. If the public only saw a happy family online, discovering marital problems would be shocking because the artificial intimacy hid the truth.

名人常在網上塑造與真實不符的「幸福家庭」形象；當婚姻問題曝光時，與公眾印象反差大，因此令人震驚。

[3] …celebrities may often perform an image or identity that doesn't always align with their authentic selves… if all the public saw was a happy family.

7 Flow Chart — ONE word each 5 marks

(i) goals   (ii) problems   (iii) public   (iv) humanizes   (v) have

The flow follows paragraph 3: celebrities represent our highest goals; we assume they are invulnerable to life's problems; the public discovers what happens behind the scenes; this humanizes celebrities OR puzzles us because successful people shouldn't have such problems.

按第3段邏輯：名人代表我們的 goals（目標）→ 以為他們沒有 problems（問題）→ public（公眾）發現真相 → 這 humanizes（使人性化）名人 / 或令人困惑：成功人士不應 have（有）這些問題。

8 True / False / Not Given 4 marks

(i) F   (ii) T   (iii) NG   (iv) F

(i) False — APA says gossip is "often" not proven true, not that ALL gossip lacks evidence.
(ii) True — gossip "may be… malicious in intention," which can be harmful.
(iii) Not Given — the text mentions cultural norms but does not compare gossip across cultures.
(iv) False — paragraph 4 says gossip "impacts group bonding," so it does play a role in relationships.

(i) 假——APA 說八卦「經常」未證實，非「全部」。(ii) 真——可能帶惡意。(iii) 未提及——沒有比較不同文化。(iv) 假——八卦影響群體聯繫 (group bonding)。

9 Multiple Choice 1 mark

Answer: B — to clarify a complex point.

"Put simply" introduces a simplified version of the questions that follow ("why do we care?"). It clarifies the complex discussion about gossip's purpose by restating it in simpler terms.

「Put simply」（簡單來說）用於把複雜問題簡化重述，幫助讀者理解，並非舉例、對比或挑戰讀者。

10 Short Answer 2 marks

Suggested answer: Because other people tell you about their encounters with someone, so you know what they are like without meeting them directly.

Dr. Ludden explains that gossip helps you know what's going on socially. You don't need a direct encounter — others' stories prepare you for future interactions.

Ludden 指出：別人分享與某人的相處經驗，讓你無需親身接觸也能了解對方，從而在社交中更有準備。

[6] I don't have to have an encounter with someone directly to have some idea what they're like, because other people are telling me about their encounters with them.

11 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: But

Paragraph 8 begins with "But it's not all so innocent," using "But" to contrast the positive bonding function of gossip (paragraph 7) with its negative motivations.

第8段以「But it's not all so innocent」開首，「But」對比第7段正面功能與第8段負面動機。

12 Metaphor 1 mark

Answer: cut them down

Paragraph 9 describes negative influence as "talking badly about someone or trying to cut them down." This metaphor means attacking or lowering someone's social status.

「cut them down」是比喻，意指貶低、打擊某人的社會地位（negative influence）。

13 Multiple Choice 1 mark

Answer: C — Learning about other people

Paragraph 9 states that "Gathering and validating information about the person being gossiped about was the strongest motivation." This matches option C. Putting down others (B) was actually the weakest motive.

第9段指出：收集及核實關於被八卦對象的資訊是最強動機；貶低他人 (B) 反而是最弱動機。

14 Synonyms in paragraphs 9–10 5 marks

(i) motive/motivation   (ii) act   (iii) promote   (iv) impact   (v) portrayed

(i) motive — similar to "reason" (line: "was a clear motive")
(ii) act — similar to "behave" ("act more cooperatively")
(iii) promote — similar to "enhance" ("promote cooperation")
(iv) impact — similar to "outcome" ("positive impact")
(v) portrayed — similar to "described" ("portrayed as cooperative")

各詞均須出自第9–10段，且為同義或近義詞。

15 Reference 1 mark

Suggested answer: Celebrities / famous people / stars

"A-listers" refers to top-tier celebrities — famous singers, athletes and public figures whom we don't socialize with in person but gossip about.

「A-listers」指甲級/List 頂尖名人，即我們不會親自交往但會八卦的歌手、運動員等公眾人物。

16 Multiple Choice 1 mark

Answer: C — Having a connection only in our mind with people we admire

Ludden defines parasocial relationships as "imaginary relationships with a singer or athlete whose successes we've followed." They exist only in our minds, not as real friendships.

準社會關係 (parasocial relationships) 是與名人建立的「想像中的關係」，只存在於我們腦海中，並非真實友誼。

17 Short Answer 2 marks

Suggested answer: They fill gaps in real-world relationships / provide a risk-free way to feel connected (you can't be rejected).

Paragraph 12 explains parasocial relationships can fill gaps in real relationships and offer connection without risk of rejection, since the celebrity isn't actually in a relationship with you.

準社會關係可填補現實人際的空缺，且因對方並非真實交往，不會拒絕你，是低風險的連結方式。

18 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: dissatisfaction / unhappiness / stress (any one)

Benning lists "feelings of dissatisfaction, unhappiness, or stress" as difficult feelings that celebrity gossip helps people numb out or escape from.

Benning 列舉的不快情緒包括：不滿 (dissatisfaction)、不快樂 (unhappiness)、壓力 (stress)，答其中一個即可。

19 Multiple Choice 1 mark

Answer: C — To help understand people's motives

The entire text explores why people gossip — about others and about celebrities — examining psychological and social motives. The writer's purpose is explanatory, not instructional, critical, or purely to present new research.

全文探討人們為何八卦（包括名人八卦），分析心理與社會動機，目的是幫助理解，而非教方法、批評現象或單純報導新研究。

20 Opinion Matching 4 marks

(i) Neither   (ii) Agrees   (iii) Agrees   (iv) Agrees

Statement: "Celebrity gossip may benefit us."
(i) Leppert — only notes we are interested in gossip; does not say it benefits us → Neither
(ii) Liner — says learning celebrities have problems "can be validating for us" → Agrees
(iii) Ludden — says gossip is "very helpful" for social environments → Agrees
(iv) Benning — discusses bonding, stress relief, safe social connection → Agrees

(i) Leppert 只說我們對八卦感興趣，未言有益→不置可否。(ii) Liner 說了解名人也有問題「對我們有驗證作用」→同意。(iii) Ludden 說八卦對社交「非常有幫助」→同意。(iv) Benning 談及聯繫、減壓等好處→同意。
