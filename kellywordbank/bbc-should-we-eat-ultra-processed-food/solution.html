DSE Solution: Should we eat ultra-processed food?
Reading Solution

Full Solution (with Explanations)

1. According to line 1, what two phrases describe meals that are not ultra-processed food?

Answer: home-cooked meals / made from scratch

Explanation (English): The opening question asks for two phrases that describe non-ultra-processed food, which the passage directly states as "home-cooked meals made from scratch".

解說 (中文): 第一段直接說非超加工食物就是 "home-cooked meals made from scratch"，因此兩個詞語就是這兩個。

2. What does "these products" refer to in line 2?

Answer: ultra-processed food (or ready meals / packaged snacks / frozen supermarket dishes)

Explanation (English): "these products" refers back to the ultra-processed food mentioned earlier in the paragraph.

3. Choose the closest meaning of "premature" (line 2) in context.

Answer: B. early

Explanation (English): "premature death" means dying earlier than the average expected age.

4. Decide if each statement is True (T), False (F) or Not Given (NG).
Answer
i) Dr Touvier's research only studied French teenagers.NG
ii) Artificial colourings are found in ultra-processed food ingredients.T
iii) Small local food companies own most supermarket snack shelves.F

Explanation:

  • i: The passage says Dr Touvier is French but does NOT say the study only included French teenagers → Not Given
  • ii: The passage says ultra-processed food contains "colour enhancers" (artificial colourings) → True
  • iii: The passage says multinational Big Food companies "still dominates supermarket shelves" → False (it's not small local companies)
5. What does the phrase "Big Food" in line 3 stand for?

Answer: The large multinational food and beverage corporations that own most global snack and fast-food brands.

6. Explain what "clout" (line 3) means in your own words.

Answer: It means power / strong influence (especially in marketing and advertising).

7. State two groups of people targeted by Big Food's marketing campaigns (line 3).

Answer: All age groups, and especially teenagers.

8. Why are Big Food companies losing market share? (line 3)

Answer: Consumer attitudes are shifting towards healthier options. More shoppers now choose fresh, organic and locally grown alternatives to ultra-processed food.

9. What word in line 4 shows strong negative dislike towards public health guidance?

Answer: disdain

10. Give two original useful purposes of basic food processing from paragraph 4. (2 marks)

Answer:

  1. To prevent food poisoning and make food safe to eat
  2. To make crops last longer through cold winters (extend shelf life)
11. What risk does minimal food processing remove from natural foods? (line 4)

Answer: It removes natural toxins that would otherwise make humans sick / cause food poisoning.

12. According to paragraph 5, why do people feel guilty after eating ultra-processed snacks?

Answer: Because people know that ultra-processed snacks are "empty calories" with little nutritional value, and regular consumption damages long-term health.

13. Name two adjectives from line 5 that describe ultra-processed food's appealing flavour.

Answer: moreish, yummy

14. What is Food Garage? (line 6)

Answer: It is a new chain of casual local eateries that serve balanced, unprocessed healthy meals to young people.

15. Give one difference and one similarity between Food Garage and classic fast-food restaurants. (2 marks)

Answer:

  • Difference: Food Garage serves fresh, healthy unprocessed food while classic fast food is mostly ultra-processed.
  • Similarity: Both cater to young people looking for convenient, quick and tasty meals when they are busy.
16. What does the idiom "everything in moderation" (line7) suggest about eating ultra-processed food?

Answer: It means it is acceptable to eat small amounts of ultra-processed food occasionally, but you should avoid constant daily reliance on it because that is unhealthy.

17. Complete the summary below with ONE word taken from the passage for each blank.

Answers:

  1. (i) locally (or locally grown)
  2. (ii) poisoning (food poisoning)
  3. (iii) consortiums
  4. (iv) innovation (product innovation)
18. According to line 8, what four methods do Big Food companies use to improve their food products and image?

Answer: (any four of these):

  • Forming buyer consortiums to source certified environmentally friendly raw materials
  • Growing fresh herbs in on-site farms
  • Running staff training programmes
  • Creating customer reward schemes
  • Investing in product innovation to reduce artificial additives
19. What does "their" refer to in line 8 ("rebuild their damaged public reputation")?

Answer: Big Food companies / the multinational food corporations that are reforming their products.

20. What is the main contradiction the passage presents between ancient food processing and modern ultra-processing?

Answer: Ancient simple food processing was invented to protect human health and make food safe to store, but modern heavy ultra-processing actually damages long-term physical health.

21. What does the writer imply about the long-term future of Big Food brands if they fail to reform their products?

Answer: They will continue losing market share as more consumers switch to fresh, organic and locally-grown alternatives from smaller local producers.

22. According to the whole text, what is the writer's overall attitude towards ultra-processed food?

Answer: B. It is harmful if eaten every day, but fine occasionally

23. Which word from the passage describes food that comes straight from local farms without factory treatment?

Answer: organic (or fresh / locally grown)

24. Why does the writer mention Dr Mathilde Touvier's research at the start of paragraph 2?

Answer: To provide scientific evidence proving that frequent consumption of ultra-processed food increases the risk of heart disease and premature death.

25. What does the title "Should we eat ultra-processed food?" suggest about most people's daily food choices?

Answer: It suggests that many people today regularly eat ultra-processed food even though it is widely known to carry serious health risks, so the question asks whether this is a good idea.

26. List three types of ultra-processed food named in the text.

Answer: (any three of): chicken nuggets, mass-produced pizza, ice cream, fizzy soft drinks, flavoured breakfast cereals

27. What irony exists in the fact that Big Food creates addictive tasty snacks yet loses customers?

Answer: Big Food deliberately makes their snacks delicious and addictive to attract as many buyers as possible, but growing public awareness of health risks means many of those same customers eventually switch to healthier natural alternatives, so the companies lose market share despite their successful marketing.
