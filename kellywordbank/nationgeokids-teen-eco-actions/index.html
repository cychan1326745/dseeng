Reading Solution

Small Teen Actions To Protect Our Planet

Text 3 — Nat Geo Kids Adapted B2 Reading

🌍 ♻️ 🌱

[1] Many teenagers think single personal choices cannot fix global environmental crises such as climate change, plastic pollution and wildlife loss. They believe only governments and large companies hold enough power to make real change. However, environmental scientists prove thousands of repeated small daily actions from young people add up to huge positive influence on Earth's future. Every student can join simple eco habits without spending extra money or sacrificing study time.

[2] The first easy area to adjust is home energy use. Household electrical devices left on standby mode secretly consume constant electricity; experts name these wasteful machines "vampire loads". Students can switch all chargers, game consoles and heaters off fully from wall sockets when not needed. On sunny days, hang washed clothes outside to air-dry instead of running power-hungry electric tumble dryers. These tiny moves greatly cut household carbon output each month.

[3] Water waste is another common unseen problem in family homes. Long showers and running taps while brushing teeth waste millions of litres of clean fresh water every year across cities. Young people can limit showers to four minutes maximum and turn off taps completely during tooth cleaning. Schools can also install low-flow water taps in washrooms to reduce overall campus water consumption for all students and staff.

[4] Transport choices create big carbon footprints for ordinary families. Private petrol cars release high volumes of greenhouse gas every trip. Teenagers can choose walking, cycling or public buses and MTR trains for short and medium-distance journeys. When families must take long car trips, organise carpool groups with classmates to cut the number of separate vehicles on roads.

[5] Reducing single-use plastic is the third key eco habit. Disposable water bottles, snack wrappers and shopping bags all take hundreds of years to break down once discarded. Students can carry reusable metal bottles and cloth bags every day, and select unpackaged fresh fruit and vegetables from markets instead of pre-wrapped processed snacks. Joining weekend beach or park clean-up volunteer groups also removes tons of plastic rubbish from natural habitats.

[6] Teenagers can also spread environmental knowledge to create wider change. Share eco tips with family members and classmates during daily chats, give short student presentations in general studies lessons, or support youth environmental clubs at school. More people learning simple green habits multiplies the positive environmental effect rapidly.

[7] Global climate issues seem overwhelming at first glance, but youth power is far stronger than many imagine. A large group of teenagers all practising small sustainable daily routines together can slow pollution and global heating, protecting forests, oceans and wild animals for future generations.
