HKDSE English Language Paper 1 (Reading) — Part A

以下為全部 15 題參考答案及詳細解釋。開放式題目可接受意思相近的答案。

Total marks: 20 marks

1 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: They are marsupials that raise babies in a belly pouch, rather than true bear species.

Paragraph 1: koalas are marsupials, not bears — they raise babies in a pouch.

樹熊是有袋動物，在育兒袋中養育寶寶，不是熊。

[1] They belong to a special group of mammals called marsupials, which raise their newborn babies inside a soft pouch on the mother's belly.

2 Multiple Choice 1 mark

Answer: A — mammals that raise babies in a belly pouch

Marsupials raise their babies in a pouch on the mother's belly.

有袋動物在母親腹部的育兒袋中養育寶寶。

[1] ...marsupials, which raise their newborn babies inside a soft pouch on the mother's belly.

3 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: They absorb liquid from the moist eucalyptus leaves they eat.

Paragraph 2: koalas rarely drink water; they absorb liquid from moist eucalyptus leaves.

樹熊從潮濕的桉樹葉中吸收水分，很少喝水。

[2] ...koalas rarely climb down to waterholes; they absorb almost all needed liquid from moist eucalyptus leaves.

4 True / False / Not Given 1 mark

(i) False (ii) True (iii) False

(i) False — they sleep 18-22 hours daily. (ii) True — para 3: 'born tiny, blind and hairless.' (iii) False — para 2: 'rarely climb down to waterholes.'

(i) 假——每天睡18-22小時 (ii) 真——出生時又小又盲又無毛 (iii) 假——很少爬下樹喝水。

5 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: Land clearing for housing and farms; climate change bushfires and drought; chlamydia bacterial disease.

Paragraph 5 lists three threats: deforestation, bushfires/drought, and disease.

第5段列出三項威脅：森林砍伐、山火乾旱、疾病。

[5] farmers and city developers clear eucalyptus forests... hotter, longer droughts and massive bushfires... bacterial disease called chlamydia...

6 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: Two fused toes forming a single grooming claw on each hind foot.

Paragraph 4: two toes grow fused together into a single grooming claw on their hind feet.

後腳的兩個腳趾融合成一個梳理爪。

[4] On their hind feet, two toes grow fused together into a single grooming claw...

7 Find Word 1 mark

Answer: reserves

Paragraph 6: 'protected eucalyptus reserves.'

第6段：保護區。

[6] ...release recovered animals into protected eucalyptus reserves.

8 Summary — ONE word each 1 mark

(i) corridors (ii) forests (iii) ecosystems (iv) woodlands

(i) para 6: 'forest corridors' (ii) para 6: 'eucalyptus forests' (iii) para 7: 'forest ecosystems' (iv) para 7: 'native woodland'

各詞均須出自文中。

9 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: Run wildlife hospitals for injured/sick koalas; create protected eucalyptus reserves; plant native eucalyptus saplings to reconnect separated wild groups.

Paragraph 6 lists three conservation projects.

第6段列出三項保育計劃。

[6] Wildlife hospitals treat sick and injured koalas... protected eucalyptus reserves... plant thousands of native eucalyptus saplings to rebuild lost forest corridors...

10 Difference & Similarity 1 mark

(i) Difference: Adult males mark territory with scent glands and chase strangers; joeys stay protected on their mother's back. (ii) Similarity: Both rely entirely on eucalyptus leaves as their only food source.

Difference from para 4. Similarity: both eat eucalyptus leaves.

差異：成年雄性用氣味標記地盤，幼熊待在媽媽背上。相似：都以桉樹葉為食。

11 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: They mark trees with scent secretions and chase unfamiliar koalas away from their territory.

Paragraph 4: males defend territory with scent and by chasing strangers.

雄性用氣味標記樹木並驅趕陌生樹熊。

[4] ...adult males mark their 'home trees' with scent gland secretions and will chase unfamiliar koalas away...

12 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: Join eucalyptus tree planting volunteer events; share koala conservation knowledge with family; avoid brands that use timber from cleared Australian native forests.

Paragraph 7 lists three student actions.

第7段列出三項學生行動。

[7] Teenagers can join tree-planting volunteer days, share koala conservation facts with family, and avoid supporting brands that source timber from Australian native woodland clearing.

13 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: Eucalyptus leaves have very low calories and contain poison; long sleep reduces energy use to match their weak diet.

Paragraph 2: low energy diet requires long sleep to conserve energy.

桉樹葉熱量低且有毒，長時間睡眠可節省能量。

[2] The leaves contain natural poison and extremely low calories, so koalas must save energy wherever possible... sleeps 18 to 22 hours straight...

14 Multiple Choice 1 mark

Answer: B — Generally hopeful

The writer highlights conservation efforts and encourages student action, showing hope.

作者強調保育努力並鼓勵學生行動，態度樂觀。

15 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: Koalas are pouch-bearing Australian mammals that live exclusively in eucalyptus forests and eat only eucalyptus foliage.

The title describes their home (eucalyptus forests in Australia) and diet (eucalyptus leaves).

標題描述牠們的家園（澳洲桉樹林）和飲食（桉樹葉）。
