HKDSE English Language Paper 1 (Reading) — Part A

以下為全部 15 題參考答案及詳細解釋。開放式題目可接受意思相近的答案。

Total marks: 20 marks

1 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: The islands are extremely far from the mainland, so species evolved separately and developed special survival traits.

Paragraph 1 explains that because the islands are so far from the mainland, most species evolved separately, developing special traits to survive the local environment.

加拉帕戈斯群島遠離大陸，物種獨立演化，發展出特殊生存特徵以適應當地環境。

[1] Because the islands are so far from the mainland, most species evolved separately, developing special traits to survive rocky shores, hot dry land and cold ocean currents.

2 Multiple Choice 1 mark

Answer: B — group of ocean islands

"Archipelago" refers to a group of islands. The Galápagos Islands are described as a "remote archipelago".

"Archipelago" 指群島。

[1] This remote archipelago is famous for its unique wild animals...

3 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: Lava lizards eat annoying flies that disturb resting sea lions.

Paragraph 2 states that when flies disturb sea lions' afternoon rest, tiny lava lizards run over to eat the insects.

當蒼蠅打擾海獅休息時，熔岩蜥蜴會跑來吃掉這些昆蟲。

[2] If annoying flies disturb their afternoon rest, tiny lava lizards run over to eat the insects, creating a helpful natural partnership.

4 True / False / Not Given 3 marks

(i) Not Given   (ii) False   (iii) False

(i) Not Given — The passage says the name comes from the Spanish word "bobo" (fool/clown).
(ii) False — Paragraph 4 says tortoises "can survive months without food or water."
(iii) False — Paragraph 6 says visitors "cannot walk alone off marked paths."

(i) 未提及 (ii) 假——可數月無水生存 (iii) 假——遊客不可獨自離開標記路徑。

5 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: Plastic coastal pollution; invasive foreign animals eat native bird eggs

Paragraph 5 lists: waste plastic pollutes coastlines, foreign animals like cats and rats eat native bird eggs.

第5段列出：塑膠垃圾污染海岸線、外來動物吃本地鳥蛋。

[5] ...waste plastic pollutes coastlines, foreign animals like cats and rats eat native bird eggs...

6 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: Sailors captured thousands of tortoises for meat on long sea journeys.

Paragraph 4 states that in the past, sailors captured thousands of tortoises to carry as fresh meat on long voyages.

過去水手捕捉數千隻陸龜作為長途航行的新鮮肉食。

[4] In the past, sailors captured thousands of tortoises to carry as fresh meat on long voyages, causing huge population losses.

7 Find Word 1 mark

Answer: rangers

Paragraph 5 mentions "park rangers" — people who watch and protect wild areas.

第5段提到 "park rangers"（公園管理員）。

[5] ...eco-tourism brings money to local communities, which funds wildlife protection patrols and park rangers.

8 Summary — ONE word each 4 marks

(i) invaders / animals   (ii) tours   (iii) charities   (iv) species

(i) "foreign animals" = invaders/animals — para 5
(ii) "guided tours" — para 6
(iii) "wildlife charities" — para 7
(iv) "natural species" — para 7

各詞均須出自文中。

9 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: Visitors must take official guided tours; no solo walking off marked paths; all single-use plastic items are banned.

Paragraph 6 lists three strict rules.

第6段列出三項嚴格規則。

[6] All visitors must join official guided tours and cannot walk alone off marked paths. Single-use plastic bags and bottles are completely forbidden on every island.

10 Difference & Similarity 2 marks

(i) Difference: Male boobies whistle, while female boobies make honking noises.
(ii) Similarity: Both dive at high speed to catch fish as food.

Paragraph 3: "Males whistle to communicate while females make deep honking sounds." Both dive for food.

第3段：雄性吹口哨，雌性發出低沉鳴叫。兩者都會俯衝捕魚。

[3] Males whistle to communicate while females make deep honking sounds. When diving for food, they plunge from heights of 100 metres...

11 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: It creates local income and provides money for wildlife protection patrols.

Paragraph 5: "eco-tourism brings money to local communities, which funds wildlife protection patrols and park rangers."

生態旅遊為當地社區帶來收入，並為野生動物保護巡邏提供資金。

[5] On the positive side, eco-tourism brings money to local communities, which funds wildlife protection patrols and park rangers.

12 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: Learn about island wildlife; refuse animal shell/bone souvenirs; donate to anti-poaching wildlife groups.

Paragraph 7 lists three actions.

第7段列出三個行動。

[7] Learn about unique island wildlife, avoid buying souvenirs made from wild animal shells or bones, and donate to official wildlife charities that fund anti-poaching work.

13 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: Tortoise groups on different islands have shells of distinct shapes.

Paragraph 4: "Different island groups host separate tortoise species with differently shaped shells."

不同島嶼的陸龜擁有不同形狀的殼。

[4] Different island groups host separate tortoise species with differently shaped shells.

14 Multiple Choice 1 mark

Answer: B — Generally hopeful

The writer acknowledges threats but also highlights positive measures. The overall tone is hopeful.

作者承認威脅但也強調正面措施，整體態度樂觀。

15 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: The Galápagos Islands host many one-of-a-kind animal species that cannot be found anywhere else worldwide.

The title focuses on "wildlife" as the main subject, implying the islands' greatest value lies in their unique animals.

標題以「野生動物」為主題，暗示群島的最大價值在於其獨一無二的物種。

[1] This remote archipelago is famous for its unique wild animals that cannot be found anywhere else on Earth.
