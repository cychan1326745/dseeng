1 Identifying Information 1 mark

Answer: E-WASTE (electronic waste) is NOT mentioned

Paragraph 1 lists: paper, plastic, glass, metal, and food scraps (organic waste). E-waste is not included in the paragraph.

第一段列出了：紙張、塑料、玻璃、金屬和食物殘渣（有機物），並無提及電子廢物 (E-WASTE)。

[1] ...you separate paper from plastic and glass and metal. You rinse bottles and cans, and you might put food scraps in a container...

2 Short Answer 2 marks

Suggested answer: People believe recycling helps the community and protects the environment.

When people sort recycling, they "probably assume that recycling is helping your community and protecting the environment." These are the supposed benefits people commonly believe recycling provides.

作者指出，人們分類回收時，通常假定回收「有助社區和保護環境」，這就是人們相信回收帶來的好處。

[1] ...you probably assume that recycling is helping your community and protecting the environment.

3 Two-part Short Answer 2 marks

(i) In 1996, the writer argued that recycling was wasteful, costly and ineffectual.
(ii) Opponents said modern recycling was still new; it would improve and flourish as the industry matured and the public learned to recycle properly.

(i) The writer published an article in 1996 claiming that the recycling process as then practiced was wasteful, expensive and not effective.
(ii) Critics said it was too early to judge — recycling movement had only just begun and would get better over time with maturity and public education.

(i) 作者在1996年認為當時的回收過程是浪費、昂貴且無效的。
(ii) 反對者認為現代回收運動才剛開始，隨著行業成熟和公眾學會正確回收，它會蓬勃發展。

[2] In 1996, I wrote an article arguing that the recycling process as we carried it out was wasteful. I presented plenty of evidence that recycling was costly and ineffectual... they predicted it would flourish as the industry matured and the public learned how to recycle properly.

4 True / False / Not Given 4 marks

(i) F   (ii) NG   (iii) F   (iv) T

(i) False — The writer says "not much has changed at all" and "the future for recycling looks even worse", so he is less optimistic now than in 1996, not more.
(ii) Not Given — The text does not state whether supporters are disappointed that the industry hasn't matured. They originally predicted improvement, but there is no statement about their current disappointment.
(iii) False — Paragraph 4 says recycling is "still typically more expensive" than sending waste to landfill, so the statement reverses the reality.
(iv) True — "The slump has forced some recycling companies to shut plants and cancel plans for new technologies", so their business has definitely suffered.

(i) 錯——作者說「幾乎沒有改變」且「未來更差」，並沒有比1996年更樂觀。
(ii) 未提及——文中沒有說支持者對行業未成熟感到失望。
(iii) 錯——文中說回收比堆填更昂貴，並非堆填比回收貴。
(iv) 對——不景氣迫使一些回收公司關閉工廠，業務確實受損。

5 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: Recycled materials (that are exported overseas).

Paragraph 4 says most recycled materials are exported, and "prices for these materials have plummeted because of... reduced demand for them overseas." "Them" refers to the recycled materials.

第四段指出，大多數回收材料出口到海外，但海外對回收材料的需求減少，導致價格暴跌。

[4] Most recycled materials are exported, and the prices for these materials have plummeted because of lower oil prices and reduced demand for them overseas.

6 Vocabulary in Context 1 mark

Answer: A

David Steiner says: "If you believe recycling is good for the planet and that we need to do more of it, then there's a crisis to confront... Trying to turn garbage into gold costs a lot more than expected. We need to ask ourselves: What is the goal here?" The context is that we face a difficult situation requiring an important decision about our goals, which matches definition A.

文中David Steiner說：「如果你相信回收對星球有益...那麼有一場危機需要面對...試圖把垃圾變黃金花費比預期大得多，我們需要問自己：目標是什麼？」這裡指需要做出困難決定的時刻，與選項A含義最接近。

[5] ...then there's a crisis to confront... We need to ask ourselves: What is the goal here?

7 Short Answer 2 marks

Suggested answer: Because recycling has been relentlessly promoted as a moral virtue from kindergarten through university. People are indoctrinated in this view from a young age and never learn the actual relative costs and benefits.

Paragraph 6 explains that recycling is promoted as an end in itself and a public/private virtue, taught continuously in education from early schooling onward. As a result, even well-informed people don't understand the real economics and environmental trade-offs.

第六段指出，回收從幼稚園到大學都被不遺餘力地宣傳為一種美德，人們從小被灌輸這種觀念，因此即使受過良好教育也不了解實際的成本和收益。

[6] Recycling has been relentlessly promoted as a goal in and of itself: a public and private virtue that is indoctrinated in students from kindergarten through university. As a result, otherwise well-informed and educated people have no idea of the relative costs and benefits.

8 Summary Fill-in (ONE word each) 5 marks

(i) carbon / carbon emissions
(ii) recycle / recycle plastic
(iii) difference
(iv) hot water / heated water
(v) coal

(i) The EPA claims recycling reduces carbon emissions into the atmosphere, so the reduction is in carbon/emissions.
(ii) The EPA encourages people to recycle (specifically recycling plastic).
(iii) The writer asks "But how much difference does it make?" → the fixed phrase "make much of a difference" means have much effect.
(iv) Residents are instructed to rinse bottles in heated/hot water before recycling.
(v) If that hot water was heated by electricity from a coal-fired power station, the net effect can be more carbon in the atmosphere.

(i) 環保署說回收減少碳排放，所以這裡填 carbon / carbon emissions。
(ii) EPA鼓勵人們回收/回收塑料。
(iii) 作者問「這會帶來多大改變」，固定搭配 "make much of a difference"。
(iv) 人們需要用熱水沖洗瓶子。
(v) 如果加熱水的電來自燃煤電廠，淨效果可能是增加碳排放。

[7] ...results in fewer carbon emissions being released into the atmosphere. But how much difference does it make? [8] Residents are instructed to rinse bottles before putting them in recycling bins... He calculates that if you wash plastic in water that was heated by coal-derived electricity, then the net effect of your recycling could be more carbon in the atmosphere.

9 Multiple Choice 1 mark

Answer: B — is not very effective

The writer uses the huge number (40,000 to 100,000 bottles needed to offset one flight) to illustrate that the carbon benefit of recycling plastic is extremely small. This shows recycling (at least for plastic) is not very effective at reducing overall carbon emissions.

作者舉例子：要抵消一趟紐約來倫敦飛行的碳排放，需要回收4-10萬個塑料瓶。這個巨大的數字說明回收對減碳的效果微不足道，因此回收並不是很有效。

[7] Here's some perspective: To offset the carbon impact of one passenger's round-trip flight between New York and London, you'd have to recycle roughly 40,000 plastic bottles, assuming you fly economy.

10 Multiple Choice 1 mark

Answer: C — the statistics haven't taken into account other facts

Paragraph 8 says the E.P.A.'s life-cycle calculation "doesn't take that water into account" — the water used to rinse the bottles, and the energy needed to heat it. This omission of relevant factors means the statistics can be misleading because they don't count all the environmental costs.

第八段指出，EPA的計算沒有把沖洗瓶子所用的水（以及加熱水所需的能源）考慮在內，統計忽略了其他相關事實，因此可能誤導。

[8] Even those statistics might be misleading. Residents are instructed to rinse bottles before putting them in recycling bins, but the E.P.A.'s life-cycle calculation doesn't take that water into account. That single omission can make a big difference...

11 Two-part 3 marks

(i) To many politicians, recycling is a moral issue, not a question of cost-benefit analysis. Supporting it looks good environmentally to voters.
(ii) Answer: D — increased significantly

(i) Paragraph 9 opens: "To many public officials, recycling is a question of morality, not cost-benefit analysis." Politicians support it for moral/image reasons, not because it makes economic or environmental sense.
(ii) A "zero waste" policy "would require an unprecedented level of recycling". "Unprecedented" means far higher than any previous level, so recycling must increase significantly.

(i) 作者指出，對許多官員來說，回收是道德問題而非成本收益分析，政治家支持它是出於道德形象考慮，討好環保選民。
(ii)「零廢物」政策需要「前所未有的回收水平」，前所未有的意思就是比現有水平大幅提高。

[9] To many public officials, recycling is a question of morality, not cost-benefit analysis... ...which would require an unprecedented level of recycling.

12 Multiple Choice 1 mark

Answer: A — the rich

Paragraph 10: "it's popular in affluent neighborhoods, but residents of low income areas don't have the same fervor for sorting garbage in their spare time." "Affluent" means wealthy/rich, so rich people recycle more.

第10段說：回收在「富裕社區 (affluent neighborhoods)」很流行，但低收入地區居民沒有同樣的熱情。affluent即富裕，所以富人回收更多。

[10] Yes, it's popular in affluent neighborhoods, but residents of low income areas don't have the same fervor for sorting garbage in their spare time.

13 Table Fill-in 4 marks

From paragraph 11:
(i) Current US recycling rate has been stuck around 34% in recent years
(ii) The original EPA goal set by Winston Porter was 25%
(iii) Some state officials ignored the advice and set goals of 50% or higher
(iv) Porter advised that no more than 35% of US trash was worth recycling

根據第11段：
(i) 當前美國全國回收率大約停留在34%
(ii) EPA官員Winston Porter設定的目標是25%
(iii) 一些州官員無視建議，設定了50%或更高的目標

He advised state officials that no more than 35 percent of the nation's trash was worth recycling, but some ignored him and set goals of 50 percent and higher. Most of those goals were never met and the national rate has been stuck around 34 percent in recent years.

14 Matching 3 marks

(i) metal → Some   (ii) food waste → None   (iii) cardboard → All

Paragraph 12 says: "It's practical to recycle cardboard and some paper, as well as selected metals and plastics... But other materials don't make sense, including food waste and other compostables." Therefore:
- metal: only selected/some are practical → Some
- food waste: doesn't make sense at all → None

第12段：「回收硬紙板和一些紙，以及精選的金屬和塑料是實際可行的。但其他材料，包括食物廢渣和其他可堆肥材料，則沒有意義。」因此：
(i) 金屬：只回收精選的 → Some
(ii) 食物廢渣：完全不適合 → None
(iii) 硬紙板：整體可行 → All

[12] "It's practical to recycle cardboard and some paper, as well as selected metals and plastics," he says. "But other materials don't make sense, including food waste and other compostables...

15 Alternatives to Recycling 4 marks

(i) Landfills (modern well-lined landfills)
(ii) A modern well-lined landfill has relatively little environmental impact; methane from decomposing garbage can be captured and used to generate electricity.
(iii) Incinerators (modern incinerators)
(iv) Modern incinerators release very few pollutants, and they can be used to generate clean energy.

Paragraph 14 discusses the two main alternatives to recycling that are actually better environmentally than many people think: modern landfills and modern incinerators. Both have advantages over commonly held beliefs.

第14段討論了兩種比大眾認為更好的回收替代方案：現代垃圾填埋場和現代焚燒廠。
(i) 現代有襯層垃圾填埋場 → 環境影響相對很小，而且可以收集分解產生的甲烷來發電。
(iii) 現代焚燒廠 → 排放的污染物很少，可以用來生產清潔能源。

[14] Unlike earlier ones, a modern well-lined landfill in a rural area can have relatively little environmental impact. Decomposing garbage releases methane... landfill operators have started capturing it and using it to generate electricity. Modern incinerators, while politically unpopular in the United States, release so few pollutants that they've been widely accepted in the eco-conscious countries of Northern Europe and Japan for generating clean energy.

16 Open Ended 2 marks

Suggested answer: The irony is that recycling is supposed to be good for the environment, but recycling operations themselves create new environmental problems: extra trucks on the road causing pollution, bad odors from composting facilities, and problems with pests like rats and seagulls. The supposed environmental solution creates its own environmental nuisance.

Paragraph 15 points out that although recycling is promoted as environmentally friendly, it has its own environmental costs: extra collection trucks mean more road traffic and pollution, and compost facilities cause complaints about bad smells and attracted pests (rats, seagulls defecating). This is ironic because the cure creates a new problem.

諷刺之處在於：回收本應對環境更好，但實際上回收本身也會帶來環境代價和問題：更多收集車輛造成更多污染，堆肥設施帶來惡臭，吸引老鼠和海鷗等害蟲。原本號稱環保的方案反而製造了新的環境問題。

[15] Moreover, recycling operations have their own environmental costs, like extra trucks on the road and pollution from recycling operations. Composting facilities around the country have inspired complaints about nauseating odors, swarming rats and defecating seagulls.

17 Short Answer 2 marks

Suggested answer: Because increasing recycling reduces the demand for manufacturing new products, which requires less mining, drilling and logging. This hurts the workers and businesses in those extractive industries, which have already accepted the environmental trade-offs of their jobs.

The main environmental benefit of recycling is that it reduces the need to manufacture new products, which requires extracting raw materials via mining, drilling, and logging. Workers in those industries accept the environmental trade-offs but more recycling would reduce demand for their products, so they would be worse off economically. Therefore they may oppose more recycling.

增加回收主要好處是減少製造新產品的需求，而製造新產品需要採礦、鑽探和伐木。這些行業的工人已經接受了環境權衡，但增加回收會減少對他們產品的需求，經濟上會受損，因此他們可能反對增加回收。

[16] The environmental benefits of recycling come chiefly from reducing the need to manufacture new products – less mining, drilling and logging. But that's not so appealing to the workers in those industries that have accepted the environmental trade-offs that come with those jobs.

18 Short Answer 1 mark

Suggested answer: According to EPA estimates, only paper, cardboard and aluminum are worth recycling. These three materials give more than 90% of the total greenhouse gas benefits from recycling.

Paragraph 17 says that over 90% of the total greenhouse gas benefit from recycling in the US comes from just these three materials. All other materials combined only give 0.2% of the carbon savings, so they are not really worth recycling for the environmental benefit.

根據EPA估計，超過90%的溫室氣體減排效益來自三種材料：紙張、硬紙板和鋁。其他所有材料加起來對碳足跡的節省只有0.2%，因此只有這幾種值得回收。

[17] However, according to the E.P.A.'s estimates, virtually all the greenhouse benefits — more than 90 percent — come from just a few materials: paper, cardboard and aluminum in soda cans.

20 Open-ended 3 marks

(Example answer based closely on the text)
According to the article, for most materials recycling is a waste of time. It is typically more expensive than landfilling, and only a tiny handful of materials (paper, cardboard, aluminum) give any significant greenhouse gas benefit. When you count the extra water and energy used for rinsing bottles, recycling plastic can even increase net carbon emissions. Overall, the article argues that the "myth of recycling" is that it is much more beneficial than it actually is.

This is an open-ended question. Any answer that correctly uses evidence from the text is acceptable. The article's overall argument is that recycling is much less beneficial than commonly believed, especially when recycling materials beyond the few that give real environmental benefits.

開放式問題，只要正確引用文中證據即可。文章整體觀點：除了少數幾種材料外，回收大多數材料是浪費時間和金錢，因為它比填埋更昂貴，而且帶來的環境效益極小，甚至可能因為額外用水用電而增加污染。

21 Main Idea 1 mark

Answer: C — Recycling is more rubbish than you think

The article is titled "The Myth of Recycling" and argues that recycling is much less effective, more costly, and more harmful than most people believe. Most recycling is not worth the effort, which matches title C. Option B is wrong because the article is not balanced — it mainly presents the case against widespread recycling, not equal pros and cons.

文章標題是「回收的神話」，論點是回收比人們想像的效率更低、成本更高、危害更大，大多數回收不值得做。選項C最能體現這種懷疑批評的觀點。B不對因為文章不是平衡地談利弊，主要是批評大規模回收。
